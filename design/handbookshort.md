Morning Star: Kingdom – The Definitive Player’s Handbook

1. Introduction: The World of Morning Star: Kingdom

Morning Star: Kingdom (MSK) is a high-stakes, simultaneous-play strategy game designed for competitive environments where tactical foresight and resource management collide. In MSK, success is not a matter of waiting for a turn, but of orchestrating a symphony of actions alongside your opponents. This requires a delicate balance of aggressive card-based techniques and "souls-like" resource management, where every decision is made under the pressure of a singular objective: being the last player standing.

The game synthesizes the deck-building depth of Magic: The Gathering with the rhythmic, punishing combat mechanics of souls-like titles. By utilizing a simultaneous reveal system, MSK fundamentally alters the tempo of strategy. Rather than reacting to a completed move, players must anticipate intent, reading the board and their opponents' hidden reserves to stay ahead. This proactive philosophy eliminates downtime and creates a high-tension psychological "fog of war." To master the battlefield, one must first master the vital signs that govern survival.

2. The Vital Signs: Understanding Health and Stamina

In MSK, Health and Stamina are the primary limiters of player agency. Because these stats are hidden, they create a tactical veil; players must navigate the uncertainty of an opponent’s remaining life and their capacity to respond.

The Health System

Every player begins with 30 Max Health. Dropping to 0 HP results in the "Fallen" state, removing the player from the match. To stave off death, players possess 3 Healing Flasks, each restoring 7 HP. However, healing is not an instantaneous luxury—it can only be performed during a "Breathe" action, leaving the player momentarily exposed.

The Stamina Engine

Stamina represents a character’s internal energy and "breath," capped at 10 points. Nearly every interaction—from swinging a blade to dodging a strike—draws from this pool.

Tactical Recovery: The Breathe Mechanic

Stamina does not regenerate automatically. Players must pass their action to "Breathe." This creates a high-stakes "pass" mechanic where the player recovers energy based on their current exhaustion level but remains vulnerable to hits.

Stamina Remaining	State	"Breathe" Effect (Regen)	Total After Breathe
0 – 2	Exhausted	+8 Stamina	8 – 10
3 – 6	Mid-Range	+5 Stamina	8 – 10
7 – 9	Near Full	+2 Stamina	9 – 10

The Final Push and Exhaustion

Players may perform a "Final Push," spending more stamina than they currently have (resulting in Negative Stamina). While this allows for powerful, game-ending plays, it triggers an immediate Exhausted lockout. While exhausted, a player is restricted solely to the "Breathe" action, making the Final Push a high-risk gamble.

3. Anatomy of a Character: Memory, Inventory, and Age

A character in MSK is a dynamic construct of "learned techniques" and "attained items," defined by their mental readiness and physical equipment.

Memory, Active Memory, and Fixed Actions

MSK uses a dual-pile system to represent a character's cognitive focus:

* Memory (Draw Pile): A 13-card deck of learned techniques.
* Active Memory (Hand): A 7-card hand representing techniques the character "remembers" right now.
* Fixed Actions: Critically, Basic Actions, Mutation Techniques, and Weapon Techniques are always available. They are "Fixed" in the character's mind, meaning they do not occupy slots in Active Memory or the Memory pile and cannot be cycled.

Players may Cycle at the end of a turn, discarding any number of cards to the bottom of the Memory pile to draw back to full, allowing them to sculpt their hand for future threats.

Inventory and Handing Rules

The Inventory is a 5-item side-deck for weapons, consumables, and treasures. Items must be equipped before the Movement Phase.

* One-Handed: Occupies one hand slot.
* Two-Handed: Requires both hands.
* Any-Handed: Can be used with one or both hands, typically gaining a +2 Weapon Rating (WR) bonus when gripped with two hands.

The Age System (MoT)

Competitive balance is maintained through Months of Training (MoT). Every Technique card has an MoT value, while Equipment uses "Attunement Time" in its Age sum. The total Age determines a character's power bracket, ensuring fair matchups in tournament play.

4. The Fundamentals of Combat: Basic Actions

Combat is a mathematical dance. Success requires calculating the relationship between Weapon Rating (WR) and the cost of physical exertion.

Offense and Damage Trades

* Light Attack (1 Stamina): Deals damage equal to the total WR of equipped weapons.
* Heavy Attack (3 Stamina): Deals WR + 2 damage.

Simultaneous attacks resolve as a "Trade" using the formula: Damage = Damage_A - Damage_B If values are equal, the attacks cancel. Otherwise, the player with the higher value deals the difference.

Defense and The Mitigation Equation

Defending costs 1 Stamina and uses the weapon or shield’s WR. Shields often grant a specific Shield Bonus. Damage_{Taken} = Rating_{Attack} - (Rating_{Defense} + Bonus_{Shield})

Advanced Mitigation: If incoming damage exceeds your defense, you may optionally choose a 1:1 Stamina Trade, burning your physical reserves to negate excess damage. However, if the remaining damage exceeds your available stamina, a Guard Break occurs. This shatters your guard, inflicts full damage, and ensures the opponent’s next hit is a Critical.

Evasion: Dodge vs. Parry

* Dodge (2 Stamina): The defender moves to an adjacent space. The attacker must guess a "sector" (Left, Center, or Right). If the defender moves into that sector, the dodge fails. Statistical Favor sits with the defender: there are 8 possible movement spaces, while the attacker can only cover 2 or 3.
* Parry (4 Stamina): A high-risk counter. If timed correctly against an attack, the opponent is immediately Guard Broken.

5. Spatial Mastery: Positioning and Range

The 9x9 grid is the language of range and movement. Players use Direction Tokens to indicate their intent in secret.

The Directional System

While movement uses precise Cardinal Directions, the game simplifies these into General Directions (Horizontal/Vertical or Diagonal) for situational checks. For example, a North-East (NE) movement is considered either North or East depending on the most relevant axis to the interaction.

Range Dynamics and Projectiles

* Melee: Hits 1 unit away (0 units between creatures).
* Ranged: Projectile-based attacks with set distances.
  * Standard Projectiles: Move during the Movement Phase.
  * Fast Projectiles: Move during the Movement Phase and the Reveal Phase.

The Direction of Defense

A player only blocks attacks from their "front" sector. Positioning yourself behind an opponent allows you to bypass the Mitigation Equation entirely, making flanking the most effective way to ignore shields and defense ratings.

6. The Rhythm of Battle: Turn Structure

The "Simultaneous Reveal" eliminates the wait time of traditional turn-based games and rewards high-level prediction.

The Four Main Phases

1. Movement: Reveal movement and execute. Standard projectiles move. Inventory/Hand organization occurs here.
2. Action: Secretly choose techniques and directions.
3. Reveal: Share the name and description of actions. Lying is prohibited, but detailed mechanical explanations are discouraged to maintain a sense of mystery.
4. Resolution: Resolve trades, mitigation, and apply damage.

The Detailed Turn Sequence

1. Memory Cycle: (Optional) Discard to bottom, draw to hand size.
2. Movement Phase: Creatures and Standard Projectiles move.
3. Action Phase: Secret selection of actions.
4. Reveal Phase: Details shared; Fast Projectiles make their second movement.
5. Resolution Phase: Effects are resolved.

7. Power and Corruption: Mutations, Marks, and Items

Advanced customization allows players to transcend human limits at a heavy price.

Mutations

Mutants are powerful but biologically fragile. All mutants suffer a flat -5 Max HP penalty in addition to the following:

* Alpha Mutations: High power, -6 Max HP.
* Beta Mutations: Moderate power, -2 Max HP.

Marks and Seals

* Marks of the Kings: Permanent seals granted by non-human King Entities. These provide extraordinary power but require a heavy sacrifice of Health or Stamina and cannot be removed by humans.
* Player Marks: Often requiring a Touch action, these temporary effects are placed by opponents to drain life or restrain movement.

Uniqueness and Stamina Influx

MSK employs a Uniqueness Rule to prevent duplicate legendary items in a single match. To ensure competitive integrity, a Judge Check is required pre-game; if two players bring the same Unique item, neither may use it. Additionally, players can utilize Stamina Influx, paying extra stamina in secret (X:X variable cost) to boost an action’s effects and surprise an overconfident foe.

8. The Pantheon of Archetypes: Finding Your Path

Archetypes are the thematic souls of a deck. MSK follows the "One Thing" philosophy: each card should perform one task effectively, forcing players to build creative "combos" (e.g., making a sword flammable before igniting it).

Archetype Specialization

To prevent "goodstuff" decks from dominating, strong techniques require Archetype Depth—a minimum number of same-themed cards in the deck. This ensures that the most powerful abilities are reserved for players committed to a specific theme.

Core Archetypes

* Darkness: High-power archetypes requiring King Marks.
  * Fearweavers: Use fear stacking and paralysis.
  * Midas: Bribes and gold-transmutation.
  * Scaled Beasts: Gain strength through successive hits.
  * Silver Shackles: Utilize long chains to constrict foes.
  * Tar: Sentient silver petrol that forms tentacles and blades.
  * Attraction: Uses deceptive beauty and "hands within hands" to enslave victims.
* Sciences: Synergistic archetypes with unique mini-games.
  * Alchemist: Uses a Crucible to brew concoctions from 3 ingredients.
  * Artificer: Combines Components to craft weapons mid-battle (items only, not techniques).
  * Wizard: Focuses on physics, geometry, and dimensional math.
  * Tech: Focuses on electricity, circuits, and plasma.
  * Sage: The "programmer" who uses Programmable Crystals and algorithms.
* The Children of Yeshua (YHWH): Faith-based authority that lacks stamina-heavy attacks but can undo marks of darkness. Sub-archetypes include The Incandescent (consuming flame), The Overflowing (living water), The Lightbringer (pure light), and The Salineous (salt of the earth).

Whether you master the technical complexity of the Sciences or the corruptive power of the Kings, victory in MSK is reserved for those with the tactical discipline to be the last one standing.
