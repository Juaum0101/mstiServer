# Introduction {#introduction}

	Morning Star: Kingdom is a player vs player strategy game where you are fighting against your friends in order to see who can come out on top. Build your character to match your foes and battle for power and glory. To win you must be the last player standing.

	The game is a mixture of Magic: the Gathering with a souls-like inspiration where characters on the board are controlled by the cards you play. The game is played simultaneously between all players on every turn, making the game fast paced and open to as many players as you want in the same game.

## Game Components {#game-components}

	In order to play the game you will need:

* Each player needs  
  * 1 Deck  
    * Check the “Deck” section  
  * 1 player piece  
  * 1 set of direction tokens  
  * 1 health tracker  
  * 1 stamina tracker  
* A board to place the players  
  * A 9x9 grid is preferable

## Inspiration {#inspiration}

	MSK draws inspiration from multiple sources, most of which are electronic games, board games or different forms of animation. The core mechanics of combat are designed to make the game a “souls-like” base, to which more complex mechanics are added.

# Deck {#deck}

## Deck Anatomy {#deck-anatomy}

Your playable character is usually a standard human, much like the rest of us. The way you develop your character is by creating a deck with the techniques your character has learned over time and the items they have attained. There are other ways to modify your character, read through this manual to learn more about them\!

Before any game can happen, players build a deck, composed of: 

1. Techniques  
2. Inventory Items including:  
   1. Weapons and respective weapon techniques  
   2. Consumables and Treasures

Optionally, they may also contain:

3. Mutations alongside their respective mutation techniques  
4. Extra basic actions   
5. Marks (if the player chooses to use those, usually require touch)  
6. Effect cards that might be created by their other cards.

## Memory and Active Memory {#memory-and-active-memory}

In MSK you have your hand of cards and a deck to draw from, known as Memory and Active Memory. The cards that go into your hand are the Active Memory while the pile of cards you draw from is your Memory. This represents what techniques your playable character knows from their training and what they remember right now. 

Your deck typically has 20 technique cards. Your Active Memory size is typically 7 cards while your Memory size is typically 13 cards. 

* **Cycling:** At the end of a turn, you may discard any number of cards to the bottom of your "Memory" (Deck) and draw back to full.  
* **Fixed Actions:** Basic actions, Mutation Techniques and Weapon Techniques are always available and do not count towards your Active Memory/Memory. Basic actions cannot be cycled.

## Inventory {#inventory}

Your inventory is a side deck where you keep all of the Weapons, consumable items and treasures that your playable character may use in battle. It has a limit of 5 items of any kind. These items can be equipped during any turn before the movement phase.

## The Age system {#the-age-system}

Deck-building is balanced by a character's "Age" measured in Months of Training (MoT).  
Every Technique card has an MoT value.  
Equipment has "Attunement Time" included in the Age sum.  
A deck's total Age determines its power bracket, allowing players to easily match power levels.

## Mutations {#mutations}

Players can modify their playable character’s anatomy via Mutations. In the world of MSK mutant humans are born with genetic modifications that can be natural or induced in a laboratory. These humans have access to many powerful techniques, but usually don’t live to be too old since mutations make their body more fragile than that of a genetically standard human.

The Price: There is a flat \-5 Max HP penalty for having any mutation, plus specific HP reductions per mutation.  
Alpha & Beta: Alpha mutations are stronger but carry heavier HP penalties.  
Alpha mutation price is \-6 Max HP  
Beta mutation price is \-2 Max HP

## Marks {#marks}

Marks are seals that can be placed on your playable character by you or by other players during battle. Some marks give you powers beyond your human limits, granted from the powerful kings, but they require a heavy sacrifice. These king marks give you special techniques associated to their specific king, but create a penalty on your health or your stamina. 

Other marks are placed on you by other players in order to strengthen them, weaken you or both. Marks’ effects can range from draining your health to restraining your movement and sometimes can be controlled at a distance. These generally require them to perform a Touch action, so be very careful when in close quarters.

If a mark is secret, you will not be able to know the mark’s name or effects, but there are techniques that can identify marks. Once identified, you may learn how to remove these marks, or they may simply vanish due to their temporary nature. **Be warned, however,** that king marks cannot be removed by humans.

# Actions {#actions}

## Basic actions {#basic-actions}

There are many basic actions in MSK, let’s look at three of them: Light Attack, Defending and Dodging.

#### Light Attack {#light-attack}

	Light Attack is the basic action that costs **1 stamina**, performed when attempting to damage a creature in range. Sum the WR of all of the weapons used in that attack to get the damage your attack will deal (plus any buffs).

##### Trades (Attack vs Attack) {#trades-(attack-vs-attack)}

When two attacks are revealed against each other, use the difference in damage:  
$$Damage \= Damage\_A \- Damage\_B$$  
The player with the higher damage deals the difference to the opponent. If the damage is equal, none take damage.

#### Defense {#defense}

* **Cost**: A flat **1 Stamina**.

* **Effect**: You use your equipped weapon or shield’s **Weapon Rating** to negate incoming damage.

* **Shield Bonus**: Shields are specialized for this action and gain additional bonuses to their Rating specifically while defending.

The Mitigation Equation

When an opponent's attack connects with your guard, the damage you take is calculated as follows:

$$Damage\_{Taken} \= Rating\_{Attack} \- (Rating\_{Defense} \+ Bonus\_{Shield})$$  
---

🛡️ Advanced Mitigation:

If the incoming damage exceeds your Defense Rating, you don't have to take the full hit. You can choose to burn through your physical reserves to minimize the impact.

* **1:1 Stamina Trade**: For every **1 additional Stamina** you pay, you negate **1 point of excess damage**.

* **The Limit**: You can only spend up to your remaining Stamina.

* **The Risk**: While this saves your Health, it pushes you significantly closer to the **Exhausted** or **Guard Broken** states.

**Example**: You are hit by a Heavy Attack (Rating 5\) while blocking with a Sword (Rating 3).

1. The base mitigation leaves **2 Excess Damage**.

2. You choose to spend **2 additional Stamina**.

3. Result: You take **0 Damage**, but you have spent a total of **3 Stamina** (1 for the block \+ 2 for mitigation).

Note: Only attacks in the general direction of your defense will be defended. Other attacks will simply damage you (being attacked in the back)

##### Guard Break {#guard-break}

The most dangerous moment in combat occurs when your Stamina cannot cover the "cost" of your survival.

* **Trigger**: If you are defending an attack and the remaining damage from the attack is greater than or equal to your remaining Stamina, your guard is shattered.

* **Result**: You are **Guard Broken**. Your defense fails completely, you take full damage, and the opponent’s next hit deals **Critical Damage**.

#### Dodge {#dodge}

	Dodging is an attempt at evading an attack entirely. When an attack is declared, the Attacker chooses one of three sectors to hit: **Left**, **Center**, or **Right**. The "sector" is an arc of movement directions that the attack will cover

* The Dodger chooses to move to any adjacent space.

* If the space the Dodger moves into falls within the Attacker's chosen sector, the dodge fails.

* **The "Favor" Mechanic**: The Dodger has 8 possible spaces to move to, but the Attacker can only "cover" 2–3 of them with their chosen sector.

Note: A creature cannot choose to dodge in the direction of a space where another creature is.

The red arrow represents the direction red choose while the green arrows represent the directions green can choose while still not taking damage

Here’s a table with a couple more basic actions summarized:

# Positioning {#positioning}

	The spots where creatures are on the board are important for things like range checks and projectile hits, therefore let’s get some specifics down.

## Cardinal directions {#cardinal-directions}

	In MSK the cardinal directions are constantly being used as a point of reference for the directions used. For that reason the cardinal directions on the game map should be clear to all players.

	In some situations the general directions are used instead of the actual Cardinal directions. The difference is that you simplify the eight directions into four. 

In the horizontal/vertical general direction, diagonal directions such as “NE” are considered either “N” or “E” depending on which is most relevant to the situation. 

In diagonal general direction the opposite is true, so a Direction like “N” is considered either “NE” or “NW” depending on what is most relevant to the situation.

## Direction {#direction}

	Whenever an action like moving or attacking requires you to choose a direction, place the direction token of your choice upside down on the table, revealing it whenever the action is being resolved.

## Techniques {#techniques}

	Techniques are the core of your arsenal in MSK, most of the actions you perform are techniques of various styles. Since they are the most important aspect of your gameplay, it is important to understand their mechanics very well.

* Name  
* Stamina Cost  
* Handing  
* Conditions  
* Effects  
* Range and range type  
* Description

### Handing & Conditions {#handing-&-conditions}

	Aside from stamina costs there is much to check before you can perform a technique. If a technique requires hands to use, these hands cannot be holding a weapon or treasure. Some techniques have other requirements, like a specific weapon in hand or an active elemental effect, these are required in order to use the techniques.

# Items {#items}

## Unique items {#unique-items}

	Uniqueness is a game rule that incentivises diversity and creativity in MSK. If you have a unique weapon or treasure, there is only one of these in the universe. This means that two players cannot go into battle whilst both having the same unique weapon. If that happens, none of the players who wanted to start with the unique weapon/treasure can start the game with it.

To prevent this, it is recommended to ask a judge or a third party to go through every player’s inventory in order to check for conflicts before the game starts.

## Weapons {#weapons}

Weapons are items used in hand by players in order to perform actions (mainly attacking and defending).

Weapons have many characteristics

* Abilities

* Weapon rating (WR)  
* Range

Weapons can be one handed, two handed, or any handed. One handed weapons can only be equipped in one hand, two handed weapons work similarly, except they can only be equipped in both hands. Any handed weapon can be equipped with one or two hands, usually giving a benefit towards one or the other.

### Abilities {#abilities}

	Weapons may have innate abilities to them, abilities that can be used to your advantage. There are two types of weapon abilities: **Weapon techniques** and **Passive Effects**.

#### Weapon Techniques {#weapon-techniques}

	Weapon Techniques are similar to Techniques, except that they are tied to a weapon. These are cards that go into your hand only when that weapon is equipped.

#### Passive Effects {#passive-effects}

	Passive Effects are constant effects originating from the weapon as long as it is equipped.

### 

### Weapon Rating (WR) {#weapon-rating-(wr)}

	The weapon’s rating, or WR, is a number that determines how strong that weapon is in general. It is the number used to calculate things like: damage to creatures you attack, damage negated from incoming attacks and even what creature deals damage if they both attack at the same time.

### Range {#range}

	Weapons can have many ranges, but generally will only fall into 2 categories: Ranged and Melee. Melee weapons may be used to attack anything 1 unit away from you. This means that there are 0 units between both creatures. 

Ranged weapons can send projectiles at a distance determined by their range (a number of units), which can also be affected by the projectile being shot from the weapon. Projectiles move during the movement phase, fast projectiles move during the movement phase and the action phase. Ranged weapons can also melee attack, but usually at a fraction of their natural WR.

### Properties {#properties}

	Weapon properties are stats that define its usage and interaction during the game.

* any-handed weapons  
  * \+2 WR while two handed  
* Ranged weapons  
  * WR \- 1 when defending   
  * WR \- 2 when melee attacking

# Player Characteristics {#player-characteristics}

## General Stats {#general-stats}

	Your stats are what define your main characteristics. They are divided into 2 categories: **Health** & **Stamina**.

* **Health**  
  * Defines the amount of damage you can take before falling  
* **Stamina**  
  * Your player character’s internal energy, which dictates how many actions they can perform before running out of breath and needing to take a break.

	Both Health and Stamina are hidden from other players.

## **Health** {#health}

	This stat defines the amount of **life** you have in you, the amount of damage you can take. Damage taken is subtracted from your health. Once that number drops to 0 you will fall in battle and enter the **Fallen** state. Your **Max health** is the maximum amount of Health you can have at any point. Your Max Health is 30 by default.

### Healing {#healing}

	Each player starts the game with 3 healing flasks which restore 7 HP each. Using a healing flask can only be done while performing a **Breathe** action.

## Stamina {#stamina}

	Your stamina is a representation of the breath of life in your lungs, or your body’s internal energy. Almost everything you do in MSK costs stamina. 

### Max stamina {#max-stamina}

Your max stamina is 10 by default.

### Spending stamina {#spending-stamina}

	Every action with some stamina cost will require your stamina in order to be used. Pay the amount associated with the action in order to perform it.

### Gaining Stamina {#gaining-stamina}

Stamina does not regenerate flatly every turn; players must choose to "Breathe". You cannot do anything else (Move/Attack) if you choose to Breathe. This creates a high-stakes "Pass" mechanic where the player is vulnerable to free hits.

| Stamina Remaining | "Breathe" Effect (Regen) | Total After Breathe |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **0 \- 2 (Exhausted)** | **\+8 Stamina** | 8 \- 10 |
| **3 \- 6 (Mid-Range)** | **\+5 Stamina** | 8 \- 10 |
| **7 \- 9 (Near Full)** | **\+2 Stamina** | 9 \- 10 |

### Exhaustion {#exhaustion}

	You may spend more stamina than you have (Negative Stamina) on actions for a "Final Push," but you immediately become **Exhausted**.

* **Exhaustion:** Cannot perform actions besides **Breathe**

### Stamina Influx {#stamina-influx}

	In some situations you may be able to perform what is called a “Stamina Influx” where you can boost a property or effect by paying stamina. Stamina Influx is paid in secret before performing the influxed action.

#### Extra stamina costs {#extra-stamina-costs}

	Whenever a creature is prompted to pay an unspecified amount of stamina (secret or public), that creature cannot pay an amount that would take their stamina below 0\.

## Hands {#hands}

Each player character has a total of 2 Hands by default, which they can use to equip their gear: 2 hands (Left Hand and Right Hand).

# Getting started {#getting-started}

	Once you and your friends have your decks set up along with the rest of the game components it's time to start the game. Since MSK is a simultaneous game, every player plays on every turn. There is no need to choose who goes first, everyone goes first.

# Turn Structure {#turn-structure}

	In MSK all turns are taken simultaneously between players. This means that every player will choose what they want to do in secret and then simultaneously reveal it to one another. All actions are then performed at the same time and resolved together. Turns are divided into four main phases: movement, action, reveal and resolution.

## Movement {#movement}

	During the movement phase, players will reveal where they chose to move their creatures on the board then proceed to move their pieces accordingly. This is also the moment where moving projectiles are moved, things such as arrows or floating orbs will be moved at this time. You are allowed to stay in place if you’d like.

	You can also change the organization of items in your hand like changing which hand has which weapon and 2 handing an any handed weapon which was being held with one hand or even exchange equipped items for items from your inventory.

## 

Note: If for any reason two or more creatures would move into the same space or into a space that another creature is in, none of the creatures move. 

## Action {#action}

	During your action phase you will can make a series of choices, these are:

* Which actions (Techniques, Weapon Techniques, Mutation Techniques, etc) you wish to perform/activate  
* What direction you will choose for everything that requires a direction

Nothing that is decided during the action phase is revealed to other players.

## Reveal {#reveal}

	At this point all players read the name and description of their chosen actions and how it affects the affected players. Players are discouraged from explaining in detail the mechanics of their actions to give the game a sense of mystery (lying is prohibited).

## Resolution {#resolution}

	The interactions between the actions are resolved and the game may proceed to the next turn.

## Detailed turn {#detailed-turn}

	Although the previous descriptions are accurate, there are in between phases which are key to the game of MSK:

* Memory Cycle  
  * Optionally: Discard cards to the bottom of your **memory** then draw back to hand size  
* Movement phase  
* All creatures and projectiles make their movement for turn  
* Action phase  
  * Choose actions to perform  
* Reveal phase  
  * Players share action details  
  * All fast projectiles make their second movement of the turn  
* Resolution phase  
  * Players resolve action effects

## End of the game {#end-of-the-game}

	The game typically ends whenever there is only one player standing.

# Lore {#lore}

The truth is that the game takes place in a "fantasy land" with some modern aspects. Something similar to how adventure time is fantasy, but it is a post apocalyptic world, so there are a lot of modern day objects around. My world is similar to that, with many different places to be and different kinds of people, in a relatively more realistic scenario, so not as colorful as adventure time. The thing is that a lot of the regions are ruled by "King Entities" who are non-human creatures who dictate how their part of the world functions. Each of them has unique powers and abilities and are absolutely immortal in any natural sense. They are so powerful in fact that they have followers who dedicate their lives to them in order to gain their powers, warlock style. The problem is that these kings are not benevolent at all. They all secretly follow a single creature, the morning star, prince of this world. He is their leader, and is more powerful than all of them combined. He has the power of light and wind and is rumored to grant any wish in exchange for one's soul. These kings and the prince of this world may give player characters special marks that cannot be removed by any means or natural purification technique. Their powers consume the playable characters, and require a high price to attain and use.

That's where Yeshua comes in. He is the true creator of this world, his love for mankind is shown all over his creation. He is the TRUE morning star, and his true light is what clears all darkness in the physical and spiritual realms. He was the one who gave the world to humans, who in turn, betrayed him and gave it all to the prince of this world. Even then, Yeshua is still as loving and will bless his children with authority over the prince of this world and his followers. The children of Yeshua have access to His authority, and although they don't have many stamina based techniques to aid them in battle, they use their faith to undo any mark of darkness and rebuke any darkness based technique.

# Power Archetypes {#power-archetypes}

	There are many archetypes in MSK, each revolving around a different theme

## Darkness {#darkness}

A conjunction of all Kings Entities’ archetypes. These don’t really synergize well if mixed by a single player, but are even more powerful if multiple darkness players work together, whether under the same king or different kings. These archetypes SO MUCH STRONGER than most other archetypes as they draw their power from the king entities.

## 

All of these archetypes require a mark of its corresponding king in order to gain access to its techniques.

## 

Every archetype is based around a way that you could be affected spiritually in your walk with Christ.

### Fearweavers {#fearweavers}

Fearweavers are spiders who follow Archweaver Ella, the queen of fear. A giant spider who poisons humans with fear for her own amusement or diet. She can weave a purple silk that carries the essence of fear inside of it, which induces hallucinations and triggers the victim’s body into paralysis from fear. Ella’s spiderlings inhabit her dark woods and are also able to weave fear inducing silk.

Human fearweavers play around stacking fear on other players, the more a player fears them, the more vulnerable they become to them. Due to being human, they are unable to weave fear themselves, so they use needles to puncture their enemies with fear which they receive from the spiders and keep in big quantities in their inventories. 

### Midas {#midas}

Midas is the king of riches who reigns half of the mountains, he is a very eloquent “human” who maintains his political connections with outsiders. He was bestowed the power of the riches, and can turn whatever he touches into nearly indestructible gold. His true identity is Mamom, god of money. Under the table, he bribes people into having things his way, and uses his near limitless supply of gold to do so.

His followers are delighted with his shining gold and are given his mark and the ability to imitate their leader’s ability. They gain the power to turn anything into gold, even other people, albeit only after repeated use of this power against that person does it start to take effect. They use the gold to draw their attention and influence their decisions only to finally turn them to gold.

### Scaled Beasts {#scaled-beasts}

The Scaled Beasts are led by the mighty {name pending} who leads the other half of the mountains. Like Midas, he is also a “human” king, but do not be fooled by this. He is known by his size and stature. His muscles are hard as stone and are an impenetrable fortress. His skin is known to hide his true form, scaled and tough he can convert his flesh into dragonic skin that protects him and gives him strength. Some even say that he is capable of turning fully draconic and that that is his real form. His reign is based on strength and power, his followers earn their place in combat and value might at any cost. 

To his followers he grants the power to converge their skin into scales. Making them more resistant and deal heavier blows. The more successive hits they land, the stronger they become.

### Silver Shackels {#silver-shackels}

The Silver Shackles are a group of humans led by one named {name pending} who spearheads the production of {concept pending}, a highly addictive substance that reels people into his ranks. Those who consume {concept pending} even once are known for leaving their families in search of more. The feeling is described as “initially inexistant, but became like a growing hunger. The type that does not go away with food. Whenever you take more of that thing it suddenly disappears, but then the faster the hunger comes back faster and stronger. Slowly you start to feel its metal grasp on your wrists, then legs, tightening every time.”

His followers are known to walk around with long chains on their wrists, feet, and sometimes their neck. ## Metafly {#metafly}

The metaflies are an intercession between humans and spectral insects with beautiful wings. When a metafly couple intertwines in intimacy, they bear a metafly larvae, a small, defenseless creature with warm skin who must be cared for until the time of metamorphosis. Metamorphosis is similar to human puberty, the young larvae must spend time in its cocoon, where it chemically removes its original skin coating and develops the initial stages of its matured body. This process is very painful and delicate, so most parents care especially for their young during that time so there are no disturbances. Interrupting or damaging the cocoon during metamorphosis could scar the young larvae’s new body, causing irreparable damage. This is why the metafly queen, Deborah, has begun works on public metamorphosis chambers, for the young in need and those who have none to care for them during their transitioning period.

After metamorphosis, the metaflies become strong creatures. Their ability to phase through reality is distinct and powerful. To them, it's as if they could see the world as a spectator, soaring through reality with their wings.

The metafly queen herself is loved by all, even if their species is not treated kindly by most humans. Her kingdom is known for its open borders and hospitality towards immigrants. Her benevolence has garnered a safe haven for all in need. Unfortunately, the neighboring kingdoms have been at war for decades. The queen’s moderate positioning towards the war irritates all parties, including the humans. All of which are not too far from marching into metafly territory for their strategic advantage.

This places much pressure on the queen’s back, as she attempts to maintain her reign peaceful in favor of protecting the people, who wholeheartedly believe in her capabilities as a ruler. What they don’t know is that Queen Debi holds secrets about her identity.

As a young larvae, Deborah was the prettiest girl in the orphanage. She had never known her biological parents, but this never held back her smile. She had a joyful attitude, and would always help instruct others around her for the better of them all. Her caretakers used to call her “Nabi”, a glowing flower native to the southeast, above the coastal region. This flower was known before then to be a symbol of great hope to the metaflies, considered a blessing to see one in the wild.

Other children in the orphanage were not as fortunate as the little “Nabi”. Her abundant joy contrasted too much with the dark past that they had. They simply could not reason with her love for life.

As she began to grow, Debi started to have many dreams where she flew around in her room. It felt to her as if she could swim in the air around her, though with great effort. She spent her waking moments attempting to fly, as she did in her dreams, yearning for the day that she would grow her own pair of beautiful wings. Once the time came, she got ready in her cocoon and drifted into its deep slumber, dreaming of soaring the skies.

The caretakers in the orphanage were always very responsible and loved the children very much. They took care so that no metamorphosis was taken lightly. Unfortunately, the other children were jealous of her and began scheming how to disfigure her source of joy.

One of them lured the caretakers away from her cocoon, as they sneaked close to access their options. Their childish minds did not yet understand the consequences of their plans, and soon enough, her cocoon had been ruptured and the liquid started spilling out. The caretakers rushed to save her life, but nearly lost her since the state of her body had almost no form.

She awoke, days later, but her appearance was irreparably damaged. Most important of all, since she was forced out of her transformation, she never developed her wings. The poor girl had lost everything. In horror, she ran from the orphanage, in an attempt to escape the pain that she felt.

Her loss carried over into her heart. No one else should be treated the same way she was treated. Without access to her wings she could neither fly nor phase, characteristics of an adult metafly. She lived hidden away in the streets of the city, wishing that at least others could see the beauty she once had.

Now, years later, as an established leader, indicated by the royal family and chosen by the people to rule over the whole kingdom, she would not falter. Her appearance seemed irrelevant to others, like some sort of magic revolved her appearance. She kept her inabilities hidden, so as to not lose the favor of the people who support her dearly. She could not fail them.
